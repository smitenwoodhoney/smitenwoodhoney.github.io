What's happening in the honey bee world: June
Honey bees may struggle to maintain their food intake and support brood production in June.
This is because their is a sudden reduction in the amount of pollen and nectar available
In the U.K., this period is sometimes referred to as 'The June Gap'.
Sometimes beekeeper's feed their bees a sugar syrup mix to support their bees through this time.
Plants which bees like and generally flower during this period include:
Herbs: borage*, thymes, coriander, rosemary
Hardy geraniums: Geranium pratense,
Veronica spicata,
helenium: Sahins early flower
Phacelia
*Amazingly, borage only takes 2 minutes to replenish with nectar (other plants take as long as 24 hours).
